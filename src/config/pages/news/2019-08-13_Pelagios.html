The Pelegios Networks has been a long time partner of the DAI linking information online through common references to places while focusing on six core activities: annotation, collaboration, gazetteers, pedagogy, registry, visualization.


As of 2019, Pelagios has moved on from being simply a project to being an own network, the Pelagios Networks, which is funded by the Mellon Foundation. Although the network does not have any funding powers, it does present itself with a constitution. The network is formed by around 20 members so far that are bound to the network by a memorandum of understanding. The former working groups are now represented in the six activities fulfilled by Pelagios: annotation, collaboration, gazetteer, pedagogy, registry and visualization.


The DAI is one of the original founding partners of the Pelagios Network and also provides one of the two activity coordinators of the gazetteer activities, Wolfang Schmidle.
